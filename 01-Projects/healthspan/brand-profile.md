## Brand Voice

**Adjectives:** Scientific, authoritative, accessible, evidence-driven, pioneering, approachable

**Tone by context:**
- Educational: Deep scientific explanations — mechanisms, hallmarks of aging, cellular biology
- Promotional: Aspirational and transformation-focused ("Transform your quality of living")
- Engaging: Conversational science — relatable pain points explained with clinical precision

**Language patterns:**
- Uses technical terms (mTOR cycling, SGLT2, autophagy, senescent cells) then explains them
- Cites the "Hallmarks of Aging" framework as a content anchor
- Balances clinical rigor with motivational messaging
- CTAs are confident and action-oriented: "Join Healthspan," "Get Started"

## Target Audience Personas

### Persona A — "The Optimizer"
- Male, 40–60
- Biohacker, quantified-self practitioner
- High income, pays out-of-pocket or via HSA/FSA
- Already researching Rapamycin, GLP-1, longevity protocols
- Pain point: Can't access novel therapies through traditional medicine
- Platform: Twitter/X, podcasts, Reddit (r/longevity, r/biohacking)

### Persona B — "The Preventative"
- Female, 45–65
- Health-conscious professional
- Frustrated with reactive conventional medicine
- Values personalization, wants data-driven guidance
- Pain point: One-size-fits-all care doesn't address her hormones, metabolism, or aging
- Platform: Instagram, LinkedIn, wellness blogs

### Persona C — "The Researcher"
- Gender-neutral, 35–55
- Academic, physician, or highly educated professional
- Reads primary literature, skeptical of hype
- Wants evidence before committing — reads every blog post
- Pain point: Most longevity brands oversimplify or make unsubstantiated claims
- Platform: Twitter/X, LinkedIn, research databases
