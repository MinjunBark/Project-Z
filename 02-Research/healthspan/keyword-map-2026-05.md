## Top 5 Quick Wins

*These are keywords where Healthspan can rank or get cited fastest — low competition, high fit, existing content foundation to build on.*

**1. GLP-1 for longevity (not weight loss)**
- Why: Nature published a major 2025 paper on GLP-1 as the first longevity drugs. Most search results are academic. No competitor has owned this from a clinic perspective.
- Action: Expand the existing Healthspan GLP-1 research article into a series. Add structured FAQ. Target "is GLP-1 an anti-aging drug" and "GLP-1 longevity mechanism."
- AEO potential: High — definitional and mechanism queries will get AI Overviews.

**2. Hallmarks of Aging educational cluster**
- Why: Zero clinic content ranks here. Wikipedia and AFAR dominate. Healthspan's entire protocol framework is built on this concept — they should own it.
- Action: Write a comprehensive "Hallmarks of Aging" pillar page + 12 individual hallmark articles. Add schema markup for AI citation.
- AEO potential: Very high — definitional question with clear structured answer format.

**3. SGLT2 inhibitors for longevity — defend and expand**
- Why: Healthspan already ranks #1. Add a FAQ section and structured data to capture the AI Overview slot.
- Action: Add FAQ schema to existing article. Write "SGLT2 vs Metformin for longevity" comparison piece.
- AEO potential: High — mechanism question that AI Overviews favor.

**4. "Does rapamycin extend human lifespan" — balanced clinical review**
- Why: The Lancet, Frontiers, and Nature rank here — no clinic. A well-structured, honest "here's what the science says" article from a clinical perspective would rank and get cited.
- Action: Write evidence-based review citing the same papers that rank. Add FAQ schema.
- AEO potential: Very high — factual question where AI draws from authoritative summaries.

**5. "What is cellular senescence" — accessible explainer**
- Why: Only academic papers rank. Zero accessible clinical content. Healthspan's Rapamycin and SGLT2 products directly target senescent cells — this is their mechanism.
- Action: Write an accessible 1,500-word explainer targeting the definition, why it matters, and what you can do about it.
- AEO potential: High — definitional query that AI Overviews generate answers for.

## AEO Content Structure Recommendations

For any article targeting AI-generated answers (Google AI Overview, Perplexity, ChatGPT):

1. Answer the question directly in the first 100 words
2. Use H2/H3 headers that mirror the search query
3. Add a FAQ section with schema markup (JSON-LD)
4. Cite primary research papers (Lancet, Nature, PMC) — AI engines weight authoritative sources
5. Include a "bottom line" summary paragraph — AI engines excerpt this

Healthspan's existing blog articles have the science but likely lack the AEO structural formatting.
This is a quick win — restructure existing articles before writing new ones.
